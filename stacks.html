Stacks A personalized money-saving tool

Overview

My objective: “Help people save money quickly in preparation for a big purchase or expenditure.” I wanted to create a take anywhere tool that was easy to use. My proposed users weren’t new to technology but they usually avoided working on their finances. I would need to find ways to personalize their experience to help them stay focused on their goals.

My Role

Brand Guidelines Document

I worked in Figma to create an extensive but concise brand guidelines document. This established ground rules for the overarching theme of the product I would design. Any time there were design questions as I moved forward this would help me answer them. I used information on the competitive market to source my theme ensuring familiarity.

Full Document

User Flow Diagram

Acting off the user stories provided by my brief I created a user flow diagram that mapped the path of action through the app that I would design screens for.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Following my user flow diagram I began iterating designs in pen and paper for each screen.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

I used the best ideas within my low-fidelity wireframes to make digital mid-fidelity screens in Figma. This made it so I could build interactive prototypes to test with volunteers.

User Testing

I turned my mid-fidelity wireframes into high-fidelity designs by repeatedly testing with possible users to define and correct pain points in my user flows and guide my final layout decisions.

Preference Test Results

A quick preference test through Usabilityhub helped me to discern which layout design people would prefer.

Feedback Results

I considered feedback carefully before finalizing my designs. My goal throughout was to create a product with wide appeal. From the start I was guided by an aesthetic that was expressed by my brand guidelines and the objective of helping people to save money for an upcoming expenditure.

Final Designs

I most enjoyed designing the UI and creating my brand guidelines document.
